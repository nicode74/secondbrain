Status: #study/learning 

## 📖 Key Concepts & Definitions
- **DevOps**: A cultural and technical movement that bridges the gap between development (Dev) and operations (Ops) through automation, communication, and shared responsibility.
- **CI/CD**: Continuous Integration and Continuous Delivery/Deployment – the backbone of automated software delivery.
- **IaC**: Infrastructure as Code – managing infrastructure through code instead of manual configuration.
- **Three Ways of DevOps**:
	1. **Flow**: Accelerate the movement of work from development to operations.
	2. **Feedback**: Shorten and amplify feedback loops.
	3. **Continuous Learning**: Create a high-trust culture of experimentation and risk-taking.

## 📝 Detailed Notes
### The Core Pillars (CAMS)
- **C**ulture: Breaking down silos between teams.
- **A**utomation: Removing manual steps in the pipeline.
- **M**easurement: Tracking KPIs like deployment frequency and mean time to recovery (MTTR).
- **S**haring: Encouraging collaboration and shared ownership.

### Benefits of DevOps
- Faster time to market.
- Improved reliability and security (DevSecOps).
- Higher developer productivity.

## 🧠 Questions & Flashcards
- **Q**: What are the "Three Ways" of DevOps?
  - **A**: Flow, Feedback, and Continuous Learning/Experimentation.
- **Q**: What does MTTR stand for?
  - **A**: Mean Time to Recovery – a key metric for measuring how quickly a system recovers from failure.
